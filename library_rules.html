Senior Library Rules

Helping everyone enjoy a respectful and productive learning environment.

I. General Conduct

  • Maintain Silence: Absolute silence must be observed at all times. All conversations must be held outside.
  • No Personal Belongings: Personal books, bags, and other belongings must be stored in the designated area outside the library.
  • Cell Phones & Technology: Cell phones must be switched off or kept on silent mode and are not to be used. Laptops are permitted for academic work only; volume must be muted, and personal headphones are mandatory for any audio content.
  • Food and Drink: Only water in sealed bottles is allowed. No food, sugary drinks, or hot beverages are permitted to protect library materials.
  • Respect the Space: Keep your study area clean and tidy. Do not move furniture or leave personal belongings unattended for extended periods.
  • Handle Materials with Care: Users are responsible for handling all library materials carefully. Do not mark, underline, deface, or damage any books.

II. Borrowing and Returning Materials

  • ID Card Required: Your school ID card is mandatory for entering the library and borrowing resources.
  • Borrowing Limits: Students are limited to borrow only one book at a time for only one week (7 days).
  • Checkout Procedure: All resources must be officially checked out at the circulation desk before leaving the library.
  • Return on Time: Books must be returned on or before the due date. Late returns may result in fines or a temporary suspension of borrowing privileges.
  • Damage or Loss: If a book is lost, damaged, or misused, the user will be required to replace the book or pay its full replacement cost.

III. Senior Specific Privileges

  • Senior Study Carrels: Access to the dedicated Senior Study Carrels (must be booked in advance).
  • Extended Borrowing: Extended borrowing limits for long-term projects (requires prior librarian approval).
  • Priority Access: Priority access to printing and scanning facilities.

IV. Important Restrictions

  • Reference Materials: Reference books, journals, and periodicals are for use only within the library and cannot be checked out.
  • New Arrivals: Newly purchased books may not be available for loan for a certain period, but are typically displayed separately for viewing.

Thank you for helping us keep the Senior Library a vibrant and peaceful place for all students!
